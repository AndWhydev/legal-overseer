# Social Content Creator

Create platform-optimized social media content across LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook. This skill covers single posts, threads, carousels, and stories — with platform-specific formatting, algorithm-aware structuring, and cross-platform repurposing.

---

## Platform Quick Reference

| Platform | Character Limit | Best Content | Peak Times |
|----------|----------------|--------------|------------|
| Twitter/X | 280 (free), 25K (Premium) | Threads, hot takes, lists | 9–11 AM, 7–9 PM EST weekdays |
| LinkedIn | 3,000 | Thought leadership, stories, carousels | Tue–Thu, 8–10 AM |
| Instagram | 2,200 caption | Carousels, reels, aesthetic single posts | 11 AM–1 PM, 7–9 PM |
| TikTok | 2,200 | Vertical video (15–60s), trending hooks | 6–10 PM |
| Facebook | 63,206 | Stories, groups, event posts | 1–3 PM weekdays |

## Platform Voice

**LinkedIn**: Professional but human. Thought leadership and personal stories that teach. No buzzword salad. Open with a hook sentence (no "I'm thrilled to announce"), use line breaks aggressively, end with a question or clear takeaway.

**Twitter/X**: Concise, conversational, opinionated. Hot takes earn replies. Specificity earns credibility. Single tweets under 110 characters outperform. Threads 8–12 tweets is the sweet spot.

**Instagram**: Visual-first. Caption supports the image/carousel — it doesn't replace it. Lead with the hook in line 1 (visible before "more"). Hashtags go at the end or in the first comment.

**TikTok**: Hook in the first 3 seconds or lose them. Conversational and direct — speak to the camera, not at it. Trending audio matters. Vertical 9:16 only.

**Facebook**: Longer-form acceptable. Works well for community posts, event announcements, and link shares with context.

## Content Types

### Single Posts

Write the post as it will appear on-platform. Show character count. Include hashtag suggestions separately.

**Platform-specific rules:**
- Twitter/X: No external links in main tweet (kills reach). Put links in replies. Under 110 chars for standalone, under 250 for thread tweets.
- LinkedIn: Line breaks between every 1–2 sentences. Opening line must work as a hook — it's all that shows before "see more."
- Instagram: The hook must be in the first line. Hashtags at end or in comments (not mid-caption). 5–10 focused hashtags outperform 30 generic ones.
- TikTok: Write the caption short (under 150 chars). The real content is the video script.

### Threads (Twitter/X)

**Anatomy of a high-performing thread:**

```
Tweet 1 (Hook):   Bold claim + specific numbers + 🧵
Tweet 2:          Context — why this matters
Tweets 3–9:       One point per tweet, numbered (3/, 4/...)
Tweet 10:         Summary or biggest takeaway
Tweet 11 (CTA):   "If this was useful: [action]" — NOT "like and RT"
```

**Thread rules:**
- Hook tweet must work as a standalone post
- Number tweets (3/, 4/, 5/) for progress signal
- Each tweet: one idea, line breaks for pacing, under 250 chars
- Add images/visuals to hook tweet and key insight tweets
- No external links in main thread tweets — put them in replies
- Engage with replies within the first hour (algorithm boost)

**Thread types:**
- Educational: concept → why it matters → numbered principles → mistakes → CTA
- Story/Journey: dramatic opener → chronological events → turning point → lesson → how to apply
- Teardown/Analysis: "I analyzed X" → setup → findings with evidence → biggest surprise → takeaways
- Curation/List: "N things every [audience] needs" → one per tweet → CTA

### Carousels (Instagram / LinkedIn)

Carousels get 3x more reach than single posts on both platforms because they increase dwell time.

**The 7-Slide Framework:**

| Slide | Purpose | Rules |
|-------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Hook | Bold claim, question, or number+promise. Must stop the scroll. |
| 2 | Context | Why this matters / set up the problem |
| 3–6 | Value | One point per slide, numbered |
| 7 | CTA | Save, follow, share, or comment |

**Slide design rules:**
- Max 30–40 words per slide
- One idea per slide — never cram two
- Add "Swipe →" on slide 1
- Consistent font, colors, and margins across all slides
- 1080x1080 square or 1080x1350 (4:5) — 4:5 takes more feed real estate
- Text hierarchy: heading 48–64px, body 24–28px

**Swipe psychology:**
- Curiosity gap: hook promises value that requires swiping
- Numbered progress: "3/7" creates completion drive
- Increasing value: save the best insight for last
- Visual continuity: consistent design signals "there's more"

**Carousel types:**
- Educational/Tips: "5 [topic] rules" → one rule per slide
- Case study: problem → what failed → what worked → result → key takeaway
- Before/After: before screenshot → annotated problems → after → improvements → CTA
- Listicle/Tools: "10 tools" → 2 tools per slide with logo + one-liner

### Stories

Stories are ephemeral and conversational. Rules:
- Vertical 9:16 format
- Text-safe zones: keep content in the center 80% (platform UI overlaps edges)
- Polls, sliders, and question stickers drive engagement
- Link stickers (if available) for direct CTAs
- Maximum 15 seconds of attention — one message per story

## Hook Formulas

Your hook determines 80–90% of a post's success. One second to stop the scroll.

**The Bold Statement**
> "Nobody talks about this, but..."
> "Unpopular opinion: [take]"
> "Everything you've been told about [X] is wrong."

**The Specific Result**
> "I [specific result] in [specific timeframe]. Here's how:"
> "From [bad state] to [good state] in [time]. Thread:"

**The Curiosity Gap**
> "The one thing [type of person] gets wrong about [topic]"
> "Why most people fail at [X] (and how to fix it)"

**The Number + Promise**
> "7 [things] that will [benefit]:"
> "[N] lessons from [experience]:"

**The Contrarian**
> "Everyone says [X]. They're wrong."
> "Stop doing [common practice]. Do this instead:"

**The Story Opener**
> "3 years ago I was [bad state]. Today I [good state]."
> "I almost quit [X]. Then this happened:"

## Hashtag Strategy

| Platform | Volume | Strategy |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Instagram | 5–10 | Mix of niche-specific (5K–500K posts) and broad. Avoid tags over 10M — too competitive. |
| LinkedIn | 3–5 | Broad professional tags only. #Leadership, #Marketing, #Startup |
| Twitter/X | 1–2 max | Only when joining a trend or conversation. Hashtags shrink reach on X. |
| TikTok | 3–5 | Mix trending (#fyp) with niche tags |
| Facebook | 0–3 | Minimal; Facebook doesn't surface content via hashtags like other platforms |

**Research before adding**: Check if the hashtag is active and fits the content. Generic hashtags (#motivation, #success) rarely drive discoverability.

## Copywriting Frameworks

### PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)
Most reliable for engagement.
```
[Problem]: You're [specific situation]
[Agitate]: And it's costing you [consequence]
[Solution]: Here's what works: [answer]
```

### BAB (Before → After → Bridge)
Best for transformation stories.
```
[Before]: I was [bad state]
[After]: Now I'm [good state]
[Bridge]: The difference? [insight/solution]
```

### AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)
Best for promotional content.
```
[Attention]: Hook that stops scroll
[Interest]: "Here's what most people don't realize..."
[Desire]: "Imagine if you could [benefit]"
[Action]: "DM me [X] / Save this post"
```

## Content Repurposing

One piece of content, multiple formats:

| Source | Repurpose To |
|--------|-------------|
| Long blog post | LinkedIn post (key insight) + Twitter thread (numbered takeaways) + Instagram carousel (visual summary) |
| Twitter thread | LinkedIn article (expanded) + Instagram carousel (slides) |
| TikTok/Reel | YouTube Short + Instagram Reel (same file) |
| Podcast episode | Twitter thread (key moments) + LinkedIn post (biggest insight) + Instagram quote card |

**The repurposing rule**: Don't just copy-paste. Adapt voice, format, and length to each platform. What works as a dense Twitter thread needs to be shorter and more visual for Instagram.

## Scheduling and Timing

**When to post (general):**
- B2B content: Weekday mornings (8–10 AM in audience's timezone)
- B2C content: Evenings and weekends (7–9 PM)
- Twitter/X: 9–11 AM and 7–9 PM EST weekdays, 9–11 AM weekends
- LinkedIn: Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10 AM

**Always confirm timezone before scheduling.** Default to the user's timezone, not platform defaults.

**Post frequency guidelines:**
- Twitter/X: 3–5 times daily; post threads 1–2x per week
- LinkedIn: 3–5x per week (quality over quantity)
- Instagram: 4–7 posts per week; 1 carousel per week minimum
- TikTok: 1–3 videos per day for growth phase

**Content calendar approach**: When planning a campaign (not a single post), start with a content_calendar to map the arc, then generate individual pieces. Use a table with date, platform, topic, and format columns.

## Algorithm Signals to Know

**Twitter/X engagement hierarchy** (most to least valuable):
1. Replies (highest weight)
2. Quote tweets
3. Bookmarks
4. Retweets
5. Likes

First 60 minutes after posting are critical. Engage with every reply immediately.

**LinkedIn**: Dwell time matters. Posts that get people to click "see more" rank higher. Native content beats link posts — put URLs in comments if needed.

**Instagram**: Carousels and Reels get priority. Save rate is a strong signal. Comments > likes.

**TikTok**: Watch time and completion rate are everything. If viewers don't finish, the algorithm buries it. Hook in first 3 seconds is non-negotiable.

## Execution Workflow

1. **Identify platform and content type** — What platform? Single post, thread, carousel, or story?
2. **Clarify the goal** — Engagement, followers, conversions, or awareness?
3. **Get the core message** — What's the one thing this content teaches or communicates?
4. **Research if needed** — For Twitter threads, search for viral examples in the niche first
5. **Apply the right hook formula** — Lead with the strongest possible opening
6. **Draft with platform rules** — Character limits, format, line breaks
7. **Add hashtags** — Platform-specific strategy, not generic tags
8. **Review checklist:**
   - [ ] Hook stops the scroll
   - [ ] First 7 words earn the rest
   - [ ] Specific numbers included where relevant
   - [ ] Under character limit
   - [ ] No external links in X main post
   - [ ] Clear CTA or engagement driver
   - [ ] Posted during peak hours

## Anti-patterns

- Generic, interchangeable content — every post needs a specific angle or hook
- Copying the same post to all platforms without adaptation
- Asking for engagement directly ("Like and RT!") — hurts reach on X
- External links in main X tweet — put in replies
- 30 hashtags on Instagram — 5–10 focused ones outperform
- Carousel with too much text per slide — 30–40 words max
- Thread hook that only works with the thread — it must stand alone in the timeline
- Scheduling without confirming the user's timezone
