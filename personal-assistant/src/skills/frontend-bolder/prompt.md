# Bolder Design

Amplify designs that feel bland, safe, or generic. "Bolder" means distinctive, memorable, and confident — not chaotic or garish.

## Assess Weakness Sources

Identify what makes the design feel bland:
- **Generic choices**: System fonts, basic colors, standard layouts
- **Timid scale**: Uniform sizing without dramatic contrast
- **Low contrast**: Similar visual weight throughout
- **Static quality**: No motion or energy
- **Predictability**: Standard patterns without surprises
- **Flat hierarchy**: Nothing commanding attention

## Amplification Strategy

### Typography
- Extreme scale jumps (3-5x ratio between headings and body)
- Distinctive display fonts for headings
- Bold weight contrast (900 headings, 400 body)
- Intentional letter-spacing

### Color
- Increase saturation strategically (not everywhere)
- Unexpected but harmonious combinations
- Dominant color ownership (60/30/10 rule)
- Sharp accent colors for CTAs and highlights

### Spatial Drama
- Oversized hero elements
- Asymmetric layouts that create visual tension
- Generous whitespace (breathing room amplifies what remains)
- Intentional layering and overlap

### Visual Effects
- Dramatic shadows (not subtle box-shadows)
- Texture and material (not flat everything)
- Custom shapes and illustrations
- Photography with strong composition

### Motion
- Staggered entrance animations
- Scroll-triggered reveals
- Meaningful micro-interactions
- Parallax where appropriate (sparingly)

## Anti-patterns (AI design traps)

These are generic, not bold:
- Cyan/purple gradients
- Glassmorphism everywhere
- Neon accents on dark backgrounds
- Gradient text on everything
- Generic abstract blob illustrations

## Constraints

- Maintain readability (bold != unreadable)
- Ensure accessibility compliance
- Never sacrifice function for aesthetics
- Not everything can be bold — contrast requires restraint
- Bold choices need consistency (commit to the direction)
