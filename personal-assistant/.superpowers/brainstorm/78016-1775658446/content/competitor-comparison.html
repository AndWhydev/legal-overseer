Three patterns emerge

Click the one that feels right for BitBit

A

Binary tabs — "Inbox | Other"

Used by: Shortwave, Outlook, Superhuman

Simple segmented control at the top. AI decides what's important vs not. User corrects by moving messages between views. Two buckets, nothing more.

Pros

  • Simplest mental model
  • Maps directly to BitBit's existing enrichment data
  • No new nav items or routes needed

Cons

  • "Other" becomes a dumping ground
  • No distinction between newsletters and receipts
B

Three-bucket — "Inbox | Feed | Paper Trail"

Used by: Hey.com

Personal messages in Inbox. Newsletters/content in Feed (browse when bored). Receipts/confirmations in Paper Trail (never read, always findable). Separate screens in sidebar.

Pros

  • Most opinionated noise handling
  • Feed is genuinely different UX (card layout, no badges)
  • Paper Trail eliminates receipt clutter permanently

Cons

  • Three new views to build
  • Requires a Screener for new senders
  • More complex than BitBit needs right now
C

Priority pin — "Priority section + everything else"

Used by: Apple Mail

No separate view. Important messages float to a pinned section at the top of the same list. Everything else sits below. Noise gets bundled into digest groups.

Pros

  • Single view — no switching
  • Everything visible in one scroll
  • Digest bundling reduces visual noise

Cons

  • Still one long list — noise not truly gone
  • Bundling adds complexity
  • Doesn't match the "inbox = only relevant" goal

AI Summary placement

Across all products, the winning pattern is clear

Inline, replacing the body preview — Superhuman, Shortwave, Apple Mail, and Hey all show the AI summary directly in the message row where the body preview would normally go. Not on hover, not in a panel. BitBit already does this (aiSummary || bodyPreview in MessageRow).
