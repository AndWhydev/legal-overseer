BitBit asks for permission.

BitBit is an AI that runs continuously alongside a business and acts on its behalf, across the channels the owner already uses, without being prompted first. By the time a client message arrives, BitBit has usually identified the sender, opened the relevant project, checked the invoice ledger, and queued the next action waiting for one tap from the owner.

What the platform does, walked through

A walkthrough: one landscaper, six months.

James is a composite, not a customer. The point of this section is to show the actual workflow the platform runs today, end to end on a single account. Every action below is in the codebase and exercised by the test suite.

BitBit qualifies him before anyone reads the form. It checks his ABN, opens his current site, sees two shared clients, and pings the owner: "Qualified lead, similar to BuildRight. Want me to handle it?" The owner says yes. BitBit replies in the owner's voice (it learned the tone months ago), books the call, and runs competitor research overnight.

After the call, BitBit drafts a phased proposal at the chosen scope and price. James comes back asking for SEO; BitBit reframes that as a Phase 3 retainer and holds the reply for the owner, because pricing is a judgment call. James signs the proposal electronically. The moment he signs, BitBit fires the deposit invoice, creates the project, sends the kickoff brief, notifies the designer, and moves James to active. Six things in two seconds, none of them from the owner.

Through the build, BitBit runs the review cycle. Designer to client, feedback back to designer, revisions tracked. The designer never sees a direct client email. The site goes live, BitBit invoices the balance, opens Phase 2, kicks off SEO. A few months later, with traffic climbing, BitBit pings the owner: "James is ready for Google Ads. Want me to pitch it?" The owner says yes; the proposal goes out, James signs, a new contract opens. The owner has not had to write the proposal manually at any point.

That is the operator pattern once the platform is running an account. Every action in the walkthrough above is built and exercised in the codebase today. First paying customers are the immediate next milestone.

What makes it different

How it differs from the rest of the AI shelf.

i.

Acts on arrival

Most AI tools wait to be asked. BitBit reads the inbound signal as it arrives, processes it against the full business context, and has an action queued before the owner has opened the inbox.

ii.

One memory across channels

WhatsApp from a job site, email from a desk, iMessage from a car. Same person, same conversation, one thread the AI keeps in its head. The channel bridges are native integrations rather than a chatbot wrapper, and identity is resolved across phone numbers, email addresses, and platform handles.

iii.

Compounding business memory

Each client's history, payment cadence, communication style, and active projects are compiled into the knowledge graph as messages arrive, rather than searched for when a question is asked. The longer it runs, the better its picture of the business gets.

Commercial shape

Three price bands.

Bit · Lite
$80–$150/mo
Sole traders and individuals. The personal-assistant entry point: anniversaries, reminders, light invoicing. The first step into the BitBit ecosystem before a business outgrows it.
BitBit · Agency
$500–$700/mo
Full ten-agent suite for agencies and service businesses running multiple client accounts. Covers the loop from new lead through invoice and upsell. Target retail-to-cost margin around 4:1.
Tender Hunter
$1–$2k/mo
A premium vertical. Continuously scans tender portals, checks eligibility, drafts the response brief. A single won tender pays for the service many times over.
Where you come in

Two things we'd like from you.

i. Enterprise pricing input

We have a defensible position on SMB and agency pricing. Enterprise pricing is the part we have not done before. How do companies at your scale expect a deal shaped: value-based, seat-based, or usage-based? What does a pilot engagement look like before a full rollout? Your specific input would be directly applied.

ii. Introductions

The platform includes row-level data isolation, audit trails on delegated actions, and confidence-gated approvals. What it does not yet have is the relationship that gets it into a corporate room. A three-way introduction works, and so does you carrying the relationship and bringing BitBit in as the solution. We are open on commercial structure and want your input on what makes sense for your network.

We are looking for your input on pricing and for introductions. The build, the delivery and the risk stay with us.
