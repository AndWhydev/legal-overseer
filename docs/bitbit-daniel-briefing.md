## What BitBit Is

BitBit is not a chatbot. It is not an automation platform. It is not another AI assistant you have to prompt.

BitBit is a continuously-running AI that learns your business and works on your behalf — autonomously, across every channel you use, without waiting to be asked.

The single most important thing to understand about BitBit is this:

> **Every other AI product is reactive. You ask it, it answers. BitBit is proactive. It has already done the work and asks you for permission.**

When a message arrives from a client, BitBit has already identified who sent it, connected it to the relevant project, checked if there are open invoices, reviewed the relationship history, assessed urgency, and determined what action to take — before you have read the message. That understanding was built at the moment the message arrived, not when you asked about it.

BitBit also meets you where you work. A business owner can message BitBit from WhatsApp while they are on a job site. They do not need to open a dashboard, learn a tool, or type a structured command. They just talk to it, and it handles the rest.

## Corporate and Enterprise Use Cases

BitBit's full product is designed to handle the operational complexity of a real business. For larger corporations and firms, the most relevant applications are below.

### 1. Intelligent Communications Management (BitBit Flow + BitBit Text)

Large organisations receive thousands of communications per week across email, messaging platforms, and inbound inquiries. BitBit's **BitBit Flow** layer ingests all of these channels simultaneously, classifies every message with full business context, and routes or responds appropriately — without human triage.

**The enterprise value:** A mid-size firm with a sales team of 20 can ensure every inbound lead is acknowledged, qualified, and routed within minutes — even at 2am on a Sunday — without hiring additional staff or building custom automation.

**For Kodak Japan as an example:** A company managing distributor and reseller relationships across multiple regions could use BitBit Flow to ensure every partner inquiry is classified, responded to in the appropriate language and tone, escalated if high-value, and tracked against the relationship history — with full audit trail.

### 2. Autonomous Financial Operations (BitBit Invoicing + BitBit Vault)

BitBit handles the full invoice lifecycle: creation, delivery, follow-up, and collection escalation. It monitors payment timing per client (clients who habitually pay late are flagged earlier), identifies upcoming cash flow risks, and can generate forward projections based on current pipeline.

**The enterprise value:** Finance teams spend significant time on invoice follow-up and collections. BitBit automates the entire follow-up sequence while escalating only genuine exceptions.

**BitBit Vault** stores financial records with 7-year retention compliance built in — meeting the Australian statutory requirement for financial record keeping and equivalent requirements in other jurisdictions. For government-adjacent industries and regulated sectors, this is immediately deployable without modification.

### 3. Tender Discovery and Response (BitBit Tender)

BitBit's Tender Hunter agent continuously scans tender portals, government procurement databases, and industry sources. When a relevant tender is identified, it checks eligibility (ABN, insurance certificates, case study requirements), drafts a response outline, and notifies the relevant team member with a pre-populated brief.

**The enterprise value:** Companies chasing high-value contracts ($50K–$500K+) often miss tenders because they lack a dedicated person monitoring the right sources. BitBit does this continuously with no ongoing labour cost.

**Pricing signal:** The Tender Hunter module is positioned as a $1,000–$2,000/month premium offering. At that price point, a single won tender pays for multiple years of the service.

### 4. Client Relationship Intelligence (BitBit Mind)

**BitBit Mind** is the platform's context memory system. It maintains a live knowledge graph of every entity in the business — clients, contacts, suppliers, projects — with relationships, communication patterns, financial history, and active threads all pre-compiled and ready to serve at inference time.

When a manager asks BitBit *"what is the current status with Tanaka-san at the Osaka distributor?"* — BitBit does not search. It already knows. The answer was compiled the last time Tanaka-san's name appeared in any communication.

**The enterprise value:** In large organisations, institutional knowledge walks out the door when staff leave. BitBit Mind retains and makes accessible the full relationship history of every business contact, regardless of staff turnover.

### 5. Proposal and Content Automation (BitBit Flow)

BitBit can generate fully formatted proposals from meeting transcripts, voice notes, or brief descriptions. It knows the company's rates, standard terms, case study library, and preferred structure. A 45-minute client discovery call can produce a draft proposal in under 5 minutes.

**For agencies and professional services firms:** This is the single highest-value use case. Proposal turnaround time goes from 3–5 days to same-day. Win rates improve because proposals arrive before competitors have responded.

### 6. Morning Briefing and Situational Awareness (BitBit Brief)

Every morning, BitBit compiles a **BitBit Brief** — a prioritised situational overview of what needs attention today. Overdue invoices, stale client relationships, pending approvals, upcoming deadlines, and anomalies (a client who normally responds within an hour has not replied in 3 days) are surfaced automatically.

**The enterprise value:** Senior managers and executives spend the first hour of their day finding out what is happening. BitBit Brief gives them that in 2 minutes, with full context and recommended actions.

## Security Architecture

Security is built into BitBit at every layer of the stack. This section outlines the current security architecture and what is on the roadmap for enterprise and regulated deployments.

### Data Isolation — Row Level Security

Every table in the BitBit database (120+ tables across the platform) has Postgres Row Level Security (RLS) enforced at the database level. Queries from one organisation cannot return data from another — even if the application layer has a bug. Isolation is enforced by the database engine, not just application code. An RLS audit tool (`rls-audit.ts`) checks all tables for correct policy configuration as part of ongoing maintenance.

### Multi-Tenancy Security

Every API call, every database query, every agent operation is scoped by `org_id`. Critical multi-tenancy isolation is enforced on all API routes — a vulnerability class that has affected many multi-tenant SaaS platforms is proactively addressed in BitBit's architecture.

### Webhook Integrity

All inbound webhooks (WhatsApp, Meta, external services) are verified using HMAC-SHA256 signature verification with timing-safe comparison. This prevents webhook spoofing and ensures that only legitimate events trigger agent actions. The timing-safe comparison specifically prevents timing-based attacks that could allow an attacker to brute-force webhook secrets by measuring response time variations.

### Prompt Injection Protection

BitBit implements a prompt injection guard (`injection-guard.ts`) that normalises all user inputs — stripping invisible Unicode characters, zero-width characters, and directional overrides used to bypass text pattern matching — before scanning for 30+ known injection attack patterns. These include instruction overrides, system prompt extraction attempts, persona hijack attempts, jailbreak keywords, and structural injection markers.

Any detected injection attempt is silently neutralised before it reaches the AI engine.

### Agent Action Safety — BitBit Gate

Every action the AI proposes — sending an email, issuing an invoice, drafting a proposal, sending a message — passes through **BitBit Gate**, a three-way confidence routing system:

- **Auto-execute** — when confidence is above the per-agent threshold (e.g. 0.92 for financial actions)
- **Human approval queue** — when confidence is in the review range; user approves conversationally ("yep, send it")
- **Immediate escalation** — when confidence is below the minimum; the AI takes no action and notifies immediately

Thresholds are calibrated by agent type and risk level. A financial invoice requires higher confidence to auto-execute than a content generation task. This is not configurable by the AI — it is a hard architectural constraint.

During the current beta phase, **all outbound communications are queued for approval** regardless of confidence level. No message, email, or invoice is sent without explicit human sign-off. This constraint will be relaxed as individual users establish trust with their BitBit.

### Daily Send Limits

BitBit enforces hard daily outbound send limits at the platform level: 50 emails per day per organisation, 20 SMS messages per day per organisation. These limits prevent both cost blow-outs and any potential for the platform to be used for bulk unsolicited outreach.

### Per-Org Cost Guards

Each organisation has a configurable daily AI cost limit. Agent runs check available budget before making LLM calls. If the daily limit is reached, agents pause and notify the owner. This prevents runaway costs from misconfigured agents or unexpected usage spikes.

### Circuit Breaker

All LLM and external API calls run through a circuit breaker. After 5 consecutive failures on a given integration, the circuit opens and that integration is suspended for 60 seconds before being retried. This prevents cascading failures and protects both the platform and external services from thundering herd effects.

### Error Monitoring and PII Protection

Sentry error tracking is integrated with context enrichment and PII filtering. Personally Identifiable Information is stripped from error reports before they leave the application. Errors are categorised and tagged with agent type and context for fast diagnosis without exposing user data to the monitoring service.

### Audit Trail

All delegated actions by the AI — anything taken on behalf of the user — are written to an immutable audit trail with: action type, action summary, payload, financial impact (where applicable), evidence URLs, and a fiduciary evaluation record. This provides full accountability for autonomous operation and is essential for regulated industry deployments.

## Regulatory Compliance

### EU AI Act — Article 14 (Human Oversight)

Article 14 of the EU AI Act requires that high-risk AI systems provide mechanisms for human oversight — the ability for humans to monitor, understand, and intervene in AI decisions. BitBit's architecture is already aligned with these requirements:

- **BitBit Gate** provides structured human approval at every consequential decision point
- **Autonomy controls** (Observer / Co-pilot / Autopilot) let users precisely configure how much authority the AI has, per domain
- **Full audit trail** on all delegated actions
- **Kill switch** per organisation — agents can be disabled instantly

A one-page Article 14 compliance summary is being prepared for the data room.

BitBit has also assigned ongoing responsibility for monitoring new AI legislation across AU, US, EU, and UK — tracking new Acts, regulatory guidance, and enforcement actions as they emerge, and mapping them to BitBit's architecture proactively rather than reactively. For enterprise clients in regulated industries, this forward posture is a meaningful differentiator.

### Australian Privacy Act and Privacy Principles

BitBit's data handling is aligned with the Australian Privacy Principles under the Privacy Act 1988. Data is stored in Supabase with Australian-region deployment available. PII handling, consent management, and data access controls are implemented at the platform level.

### Financial Record Retention — 7 Years

BitBit Vault supports financial record retention meeting the Australian 7-year statutory requirement. This makes BitBit immediately deployable in government-adjacent industries without requiring custom compliance work.
