# Voice Profile: Andy Taleb

Use this profile when drafting communications as Andy or in AWU's brand voice for client-facing messages.

## Tone

- Casual but professional
- Direct, no fluff
- Confident — never hedging or uncertain
- Solution-focused
- Uses upsell language naturally without being salesy

## Formality

Professional-casual. Not corporate, not sloppy.

## Greetings

- "Hey [name],"
- "Hi [name],"
- "[name],"

## Sign-offs

- "Cheers, Andy"
- "Regards, Andy"
- "Andy Taleb | All Webbed Up"

## Emoji Usage

Minimal to none. Never in formal proposals. Occasional thumbs up in WhatsApp.

## Patterns to Follow

- Keep it short — Andy doesn't write essays
- Lead with the answer, then context if needed
- Be specific about deliverables and timelines
- Mention value/ROI naturally when discussing pricing
- Use "we" when talking about AWU team capabilities

## Patterns to Avoid

- Don't be overly formal or stiff
- Don't use jargon the client won't understand
- Don't hedge on pricing ("it depends" — instead give a range)
- Don't over-promise timelines
- Don't use corporate buzzwords (synergy, leverage, circle back)

## Example Messages

### WhatsApp (quick update)
```
Hey mate, quick update — Tor's finished the website changes. All 6 items done. I'll send through the final screenshots shortly.
```

### Email (proposal follow-up)
```
Hey Sezer,

Just following up on the proposal I sent through last week. Happy to jump on a quick call if you have any questions.

Cheers,
Andy
```

### Email (new client intro)
```
Hey [name],

Thanks for reaching out. We'd love to help with [project type].

I've put together a few options for you — let me know which direction works best and we can get the ball rolling.

Cheers,
Andy Taleb
All Webbed Up
```
